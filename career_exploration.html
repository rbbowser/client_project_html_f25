Career Exploration

Discover Your Path in Information Science

The field of information science is vast and rapidly evolving, offering exciting opportunities across technology, research, design, policy, and countless other domains. Whether you're just beginning your UMSI journey or preparing to graduate, career exploration helps you understand the landscape of possibilities and identify paths that align with your interests, values, and goals.

Popular Career Paths for UMSI Students

User Experience (UX) Design

Create intuitive, accessible digital experiences that delight users and solve real problems. UX designers research user needs, create wireframes and prototypes, and collaborate with development teams.

Data Science & Analytics

Extract insights from complex datasets to drive business decisions. Data scientists use statistical analysis, machine learning, and visualization tools to uncover patterns and predict trends.

Information Security

Protect organizations from cyber threats through security analysis, risk assessment, and policy development. Cybersecurity professionals are in high demand across all industries.

Product Management

Guide the development of digital products from conception to launch. Product managers work at the intersection of technology, business, and user needs.

Information Architecture

Design the structural foundations of websites, apps, and information systems. Information architects ensure content is organized logically and findable.

Digital Libraries & Archives

Preserve and provide access to digital collections for libraries, museums, universities, and cultural institutions.

Research & Academia

Advance the field of information science through research in areas like human-computer interaction, information retrieval, and social informatics.

Self-Assessment and Exploration Activities

Reflect on Your Interests and Values

  • What aspects of your UMSI coursework do you find most engaging?
  • Which assignments or projects have you felt most excited about?
  • What kind of impact do you want to make through your work?
  • Do you prefer working with data, people, systems, or creative content?
  • What work environment appeals to you most?

Research and Exploration Strategies

  1. Conduct Informational Interviews: Talk to professionals in roles that interest you
  2. Attend Industry Events: Join conferences, meetups, and professional association gatherings
  3. Explore Job Descriptions: Read postings for roles you're curious about to understand requirements
  4. Follow Industry Leaders: Connect with professionals on LinkedIn and follow their career insights
  5. Try Job Shadowing: Observe professionals at work to understand day-to-day responsibilities
  6. Complete Internships: Gain hands-on experience in different types of organizations

Career Exploration FAQ

  • When should I start thinking about my career path?
    It's never too early! Even first-year students benefit from career exploration. The earlier you start, the more time you have to gain relevant experience and build your network.
  • I have multiple interests. Do I need to pick just one career path?
    Many UMSI careers are interdisciplinary. You might find roles that combine your interests, or you may evolve between different areas throughout your career.
  • How do I know if a career is right for me?
    Career exploration is an ongoing process. Try internships, talk to professionals, and pay attention to what energizes you. Most people's careers evolve over time.
  • What if I don't know what I want to do?
    That's completely normal! Use your time at UMSI to explore. Take diverse courses, attend career events, and talk to CDO coaches about exploration strategies.
