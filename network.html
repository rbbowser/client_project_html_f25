Networking & Building Connections

Build Your Professional Network

Networking is one of the most powerful tools for career success, yet many students find it intimidating. At UMSI, we believe networking is simply about building genuine professional relationships that benefit both you and others in your field. Whether you're connecting with alumni, industry professionals, or peers, these resources will help you approach networking with confidence and purpose.

Strong professional networks open doors to opportunities that might never be posted publicly. They provide insights into career paths, company cultures, and industry trends. Most importantly, they connect you with people who can offer guidance, mentorship, and support throughout your career journey.

Getting Started with Networking

  1. Start with Your Existing Network: Begin with classmates, professors, and people you already know professionally.
  2. Define Your Goals: Are you exploring career paths, seeking internships, or looking for mentorship?
  3. Research Before You Reach Out: Learn about the person and their work before making contact.
  4. Prepare Your Introduction: Practice a brief, compelling way to introduce yourself and your interests.
  5. Follow Up and Stay Connected: Networking is about building relationships, not just making contacts.

Networking in Action

Types of Professional Connections

  • Alumni Networks: UMSI graduates working in your areas of interest
  • Industry Professionals: People currently working in roles or companies you're curious about
  • Peer Networks: Classmates and students in related programs
  • Faculty and Staff: Professors and university professionals with industry experience
  • Online Communities: Professional groups on LinkedIn, Slack, and industry-specific platforms

Best Practices for Professional Outreach

  • Be specific about what you're seeking and why
  • Respect people's time - suggest brief meetings (15-20 minutes)
  • Offer to meet virtually or at their convenience
  • Come prepared with thoughtful questions
  • Always follow up with a thank you note
  • Look for ways to give back and help others

Networking FAQ

  • I'm introverted. How can I network effectively?
    Focus on one-on-one conversations rather than large events. Online networking through LinkedIn can be less intimidating, and informational interviews allow for deeper connections.
  • What do I say when reaching out to someone I don't know?
    Be honest about your connection (UMSI student, found them through LinkedIn, etc.), specific about your ask, and concise in your message. Check our email templates for examples.
  • How do I maintain professional relationships over time?
    Send periodic updates about your progress, share relevant articles or opportunities, and offer help when you can. Quality matters more than frequency.
  • When should I start networking?
    Now! Networking is most effective when you're not desperately job searching. Start building relationships early in your academic program.
